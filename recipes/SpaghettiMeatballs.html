Spaghetti And Meatballs

Description

Pasta has existed in Italy since at least the 12th century, and spaghetti (from the word spago, meaning "thin string") became a staple by the 19th century. Spaghetti was usually served with simple tomato sauces, especially in the south of Italy where tomatoes were abundant. Between the late 1800s and early 1900s, millions of Italian immigrants, primarily from Southern Italy, moved to the United States. In America, they found cheaper meat, canned tomatoes, and dried pasta, all readily available. These ingredients came together to form spaghetti and meatballs. This dish that didn't exist in Italy but quickly became a staple of Italian-American cooking.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Ground Meat
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Garlic
  • Herbs
  • Salt And Pepper
  • Milk
  • Tomato Marinara Sauce

Steps

  1. Prepare the Tomato Mariana Sauce with canned whole tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, onion, fresh basil, sugar, salt and pepper.
  2. Make the meatballs by combining the meat, Breadcrumbs, eggs, cheese, garlic, herbs and seasonings in a bowl.
  3. Form the mixture into balls and either pan-fry or bake them until golden and cooked through.
  4. Cook the spaghetti by boiling it. After it is done cooking, drain it.
  5. Assemble and toss the cooked spaghetti in the mariana sauce, then top with meatballs.
