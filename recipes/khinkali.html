Description

Khinkali has deep roots in Georgian culinary history, believed to have originated in the mountainous regions of Pshavi, Mtiuleti, and Khevsureti in the north of Georgia. Traditionally, it was a practical and hearty food for shepherds and travelers. It was easy to carry, filling, and made from simple, locally available ingredients like flour, meat, and spices. Over time, khinkali spread across Georgia and evolved from a rural staple into a national symbol of hospitality and comfort.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Meat
  • Aromatic spices

    • Steps

    1. Prepare the dough with flour,water, and a pinch of salt.
    2. Rest the dough.
    3. Mix the filling using ground beef or pork.
    4. Add chopped onions,garlic,parsley,salt, and pepper to the filling.
    5. Add broth to the filling.
    6. Roll the dough once it is ready, cut it into small circles.
    7. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle.
    8. Pinching them at the top to ensure that the khinkali is sealed.
    9. Boil the khinkali in salted water until they float and cook for a few more minutes.
